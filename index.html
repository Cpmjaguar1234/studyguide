Problem 2: Tangent Justification

Determine whether $\overline{HI}$ is tangent to $\text{Circle G}$. Justify your answer.

Answer: Yes, $\overline{HI}$ is tangent.

Justification: A line is tangent to a circle if and only if it is perpendicular to the radius at the point of tangency. We must check if $\triangle HGI$ is a right triangle, with the right angle at G.

We can use the converse of the Pythagorean Theorem:

$a^2 + b^2 \stackrel{?}{=} c^2$

$(GI)^2 + (HI)^2 \stackrel{?}{=} (HG)^2$

$9^2 + 40^2 \stackrel{?}{=} 41^2$

$81 + 1600 \stackrel{?}{=} 1681$

$1681 = 1681$

Since the theorem holds true, $\triangle HGI$ is a right triangle and $\angle HGI$ is a right angle. Therefore, $\overline{HI} \perp \overline{GI}$, making $\overline{HI}$ tangent to $\text{Circle G}$.

Problem 3: Find $x$

Find $x$. Assume that segments that appear to be tangent are tangent.

Answer: $x = 8$

Justification: If two segments from the same exterior point are tangent to a circle, then they are congruent.

$\overline{PQ} \cong \overline{PR}$

$3x - 6 = x + 10$

$2x - 6 = 10 \text{ (Subtract } x \text{ from both sides)}$

$2x = 16 \text{ (Add } 6 \text{ to both sides)}$

$x = 8 \text{ (Divide by } 2 \text{)}$

Problem 4: Equation of a Circle

Write the equation of the circle with its center at $(4, 3)$ and a radius of $9$.

Answer: $(x - 4)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 81$

Justification: The standard equation for a circle is:

$(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$

Where $(h, k)$ is the center and $r$ is the radius.

Substitute the given values: $(h, k) = (4, 3)$ and $r = 9$.

$(x - 4)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 9^2$

$(x - 4)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 81$
